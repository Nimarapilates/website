# Nimara Studio Playlist — Setup Guide

## 1. Create the account

- Sign up for **Spotify for Business** (via [Soundtrack Your Brand](https://www.soundtrackyourbrand.com/) or similar) — this covers the commercial licence. Playing a personal Spotify account in a commercial space is against Spotify's terms and risks SGAE fines in Spain.
- Alternatively, use a **personal Spotify Premium** account *only* for curation/branding, then stream through a licensed provider in-studio.

## 2. Playlist structure

Create **one master playlist** and **3 variants** for different studio moments:

| Playlist | Vibe | BPM range | When |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nimara — Studio** | Ambient, warm, unhurried | 60–90 | Default, all day |
| **Nimara — Before Class** | Soft arrival energy, grounding | 50–70 | 15 min before class |
| **Nimara — During Class** | Rhythmic but calm, no lyrics | 70–100 | Instructor controls |
| **Nimara — After Class** | Cool-down, nature sounds, spacious | 40–65 | Post-session wind-down |

## 3. Sound direction (matching the brand)

Think: **oak floors, morning light, linen, breath**

- **Instruments:** Piano, acoustic guitar, soft synths, strings, hang drum, kalimba
- **Textures:** Nature field recordings (water, birdsong, wind), vinyl crackle, room tone
- **No:** Lyrics in most tracks (occasional soft vocals OK), percussion-heavy beats, anything "gym"
- **Reference artists:**
  - Olafur Arnalds
  - Nils Frahm
  - Ludovico Einaudi
  - Bonobo (ambient/downtempo tracks)
  - Tycho (ambient works)
  - Brian Eno (Music for Airports, Ambient 1-4)
  - Hania Rani
  - Joep Beving
  - Kiasmos (slower tracks)
  - Parra for Cuva
  - Monolink (acoustic versions)
  - Portico Quartet

## 4. Curation rules

- **3-4 hours minimum** per playlist (avoids noticeable repeats in a half-day)
- **No track under 2 minutes** — short tracks break the flow
- **Crossfade 8-12 seconds** in Spotify settings
- **Shuffle ON** but curate the first 5 tracks carefully (they set the tone if shuffle is off)
- **Review monthly** — remove anything that feels wrong after hearing it in-studio, add 5-10 new tracks
- **Volume:** Background level only. If you can hear individual notes clearly from the reception desk, it's too loud.

## 5. Branding the playlist

- **Name:** `Nimara — Studio` (or `Nimara — Reformer Pilates`)
- **Cover image:** Use the brand palette — a sage green square with "NIMARA" in Cormorant Garamond, light weight.
- **Description:** "The sound of Nimara. Ambient, warm, unhurried — curated for breath, movement, and stillness. Santa Catalina, Palma de Mallorca."
- Make it **public** — clients will follow it, which builds brand affinity outside the studio

## 6. In-studio setup

- Dedicated iPad or phone logged into the business streaming account
- Connected to a Sonos, Marshall, or similar speaker system
- **Two zones** if possible: reception (softer, lower volume) and studio floor (instructor-controlled)
- Set a default playlist that auto-plays when the studio opens — staff shouldn't need to think about it

## 7. Promote it

- Link in the **footer** or **studio page** of the website
- Share on **Instagram Stories** periodically ("This week's studio soundtrack")
- Include in the **post-class email** ("Take the studio home — listen to our playlist")
- QR code at reception
